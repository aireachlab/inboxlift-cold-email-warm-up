Frequently Asked Questions

Why do my cold emails land in spam?

If you're emailing from a brand-new domain, email providers treat you as suspicious. InboxLift warms up your inbox gradually to build trust.

What is email warm-up and do I need it?

Email warm-up means sending low-risk emails from your domain before launching real outreach. If your domain is new, it’s essential.

How does InboxLift actually work?

InboxLift sends automated warm-up emails between real accounts to simulate natural behavior and build sender reputation.

Can I use this with Gmail or Outlook?

Yes — InboxLift supports major providers like Gmail and Outlook with easy integration.

How long should I warm up my inbox before sending real cold emails?

Most users see safe results in 2–4 weeks, depending on sending volume. InboxLift tracks your reputation and gives green-light signals.

What happens if I skip warm-up?

You risk hitting spam folders from day one. That damages your domain and lowers future deliverability — even for good messages.
